Introducing temp-o

Temp-o is a compact device you can place in your instrument case, in your practice room or even within large instruments like pianos. Working with the temp-o app, you can then keep track of the local temperature and humidity of your instruments! Another inbuilt feature included with temp-o is impact detection. If your instrument is dropped or mishandled at any time, temp-o will recognise it and send you an urgent notification!

When you're performing at the top level, you cannot afford to expose your instruments to harmful conditions. Instruments can be damaged by humidity and temperature as natural materials like wood warp and metals like brass tarnish. This leads to the sound being dulled, or worse, cracking and joint failure!

With profiles for a whole orchestra of instruments, temp-o provides one solution to caring for them all!

How should I keep my instrument?

  • Strings
    String instruments are susceptible to warping and cracking of their bodies.
    ×

    Violin family:

    Violins, Violas, Cellos and Basses should be kept in a moderate environment with a stable temperature and relative humidity between 40 and 60%.

    Guitar family:

    Classical Guitars, Ukuleles and Electric Guitars and Basses should be kept at a temperature between 21 and 24°C, and relative humidity between 40 and 50%.

    Pianos:

    Pianos will last longest in environments kept at 20°C with relative humidity between 45 and 70%.

  • Brass & Woodwind
    Brass instruments can withstand a wide range of temperatures but high humidities risk tarnishing.
    ×

    Brass Family

    Brass instruments are resilient to a wide range of temperatures i.e. 10 to 30°C, their humidity should be kept low to suppress tarnishing or mould growth.

    Woodwind Family

    It is well-known that the temperature of woodwind instruments affects their pitch and that the instrument should be warmed up before performing. For humidity however, it is recommended to store them at 45 - 55% relative humidity.

  • Percussion
    Drums and percussives are vulnerable to rust and delamination of their shells.
    ×

    Drums

    High humidity will accelerate rusting on the lugs and tension rods and can delaminate or warp the shells. Sharp temperature fluctuations can lead to cracks due to the expansion and contraction. Keep humidity between 40 and 50%, drums are generally hardy in most temperatures but sharp fluctuations risk damage.
